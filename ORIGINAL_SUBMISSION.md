## Idea
Project Title: FoodBridge — Smart Surplus Food Distribution Platform
1. The Problem
Hotels, restaurants, bakeries, and event venues generate large quantities of surplus food every day, but there is no reliable, verified system connecting this surplus to NGOs that could distribute it to people in need. Donations happen informally, if at all — with no way to verify food safety, no tracking of pickup or delivery, and no accountability once food leaves the donor's premises. This trust gap causes massive food wastage on one side, while NGOs struggle to find consistent, safe, and timely donations on the other.
2. The Solution
FoodBridge connects hotels/restaurants and NGOs through a verified, AI-driven donation pipeline instead of ad-hoc, untracked handoffs. Hotels and NGOs both go through a structured registration and document-verification process (business license, FSSAI, NGO registration, admin approval) before they can participate, ensuring every actor on the platform is legitimate.
When a hotel lists surplus food (quantity, category, expiry, pickup window), an AI engine validates food safety, prioritizes by expiry urgency, and matches the donation to the nearest suitable NGO with optimized routing. Once accepted, a volunteer is assigned, and the handoff is secured with a two-step verification system: OTP/QR code confirmation at pickup, and a second OTP plus photo proof at delivery, with GPS tracking throughout. This closes the accountability loop that informal donation systems leave wide open.
3. Expected Outcomes

Real-Time Impact Tracking: A live dashboard showing total donations, meals distributed, food waste reduced, and CO₂ emissions saved — visible to hotels, NGOs, and administrators.
Verified, Accountable Handoffs: Two-step OTP + photo verification and GPS logging eliminate disputes over whether food was actually collected and delivered.
Grassroots Nutrition Access: A transparent, trackable channel that gets surplus food to beneficiaries quickly instead of it going to waste.

4. Optimized Resource Solution
An AI-based matching and routing engine ensures surplus food is never dumped onto the nearest NGO by default — it factors in expiry urgency, NGO storage/refrigeration capacity, service radius, and volunteer availability to route each donation to the NGO best equipped to use it before it spoils. This turns food redistribution from a reactive, first-come-first-served scramble into a data-driven allocation system, maximizing the number of meals actually saved rather than just the number of donations logged.
